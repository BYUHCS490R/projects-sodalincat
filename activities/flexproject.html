Siemreap, Cambodia

When the morning sun shines on the old temples and ruins of Angkor Wat, the sunrise in Siem Reap feels special. These ancient buildings are part of one of the world’s biggest religious sites. Angkor Wat and the 12th-century royal city Angkor Thom are the main places to visit in Siem Reap. You can also learn about Cambodia’s history at the Cambodian Cultural Village and enjoy shopping, food, and bars at the Angkor Night Market.

Phnom Bakheng

Phnom Bakheng is a Hindu temple shaped like a mountain in Siem Reap, Cambodia. It was built for the god Shiva in the late 9th century by King Yasovarman. The temple sits on a hill and is now a famous place for tourists to watch the sunset and see Angkor Wat, which is about 1.5 km away in the jungle.
